Common Deleterious SARS-CoV-2 Mutations in Deceased COVID-19 Patients

Published: August 5, 2025

Our published article in Scientific Reports (2023) explored key mutations in SARS-CoV-2 genomes from deceased patients across various continents. We identified common deleterious mutations that may have contributed to disease severity and lethality.

Study Highlights

  • Comprehensive genomic analysis of 100+ SARS-CoV-2 samples from deceased patients
  • Mutation calling and annotation performed using tools like Nextclade, CoV-GLUE, and SnpEff
  • Focus on non-synonymous mutations affecting viral fitness and immune evasion
  • Cross-continental comparison revealed convergence on several high-risk mutations

Key Mutations Identified

Notable deleterious mutations were found in the spike (S), ORF3a, and NSP regions. Some mutations showed evidence of strong positive selection.

Implications

These findings support the need for global genomic surveillance and may aid in understanding mechanisms of increased pathogenicity and vaccine escape potential.
