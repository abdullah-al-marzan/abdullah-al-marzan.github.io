Gene Expression Analysis in Mouse Brain at M11.5 Stage

Published: August 5, 2025

In this post, I describe our investigation into gene expression changes in the developing mouse brain at embryonic stage M11.5. This project allowed us to explore the transcriptomic landscape during a critical window of neurodevelopment.

Objective

We aimed to identify differentially expressed genes and enriched pathways that regulate early neurogenesis and cell fate determination in the mouse brain.

Approach

  • RNA-seq data was obtained from publicly available datasets and preprocessed with FastQC and Trimmomatic
  • Reads were aligned using STAR, and quantification was done via featureCounts
  • Differential expression was analyzed using DESeq2 in R
  • GO and KEGG pathway enrichment analyses were performed

Key Insights

We discovered several transcription factors and signaling molecules involved in brain patterning, neurogenesis, and synaptic development. The Wnt and Notch signaling pathways were notably enriched among upregulated genes.

Significance

This analysis lays the foundation for functional studies of brain development and may have implications in understanding neurodevelopmental disorders.
