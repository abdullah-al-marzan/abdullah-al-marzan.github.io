Unlocking the Potential of miRNA in HIV Therapeutics

August 3, 2025

HIV continues to pose a global health challenge despite remarkable advancements in antiretroviral therapy (ART). However, limitations such as viral persistence and latent reservoirs demand alternative approaches.

One promising avenue involves targeting the Rev Response Element (RRE) â€“ a crucial RNA structure in the HIV genome that enables the export of unspliced viral RNA transcripts via the Rev protein.

My recent work explores the use of microRNAs (miRNAs) to competitively bind with RRE and hinder Rev interaction, potentially disrupting viral replication.

By applying secondary structure prediction tools and deep learning models trained on miRNA-target interactions, we can design artificial miRNAs that structurally and energetically outcompete Rev binding. This approach may lay the foundation for a new generation of RNA-based HIV therapies.
